Registered leases

Download data about leasehold properties in England and Wales.

Example of Registered leases data

The data shown in this example is not real data. This example is to show what you will see if you download the file.

Data What this means Example Data 1
Property description Description of the leased land, for example an address 1 Smith Street 
Region and county Location of the leasehold property Region
Unique property reference number (UPRN) Number used by HM Land Registry to identify location of the land, similar to postcode but more specific abc00000003
Unique identifier   Number used by HM Land Registry to identify the land without a title number 123456
Date of lease Date (lease started) Date
Term How long the lease lasts for 999 years
Tenure How the land is owned in a leasehold capacity (Absolute, Good, Possessory or Qualified.) Leasehold
Alienation cause indicator Clause in the leasehold agreement between the landlord and their tenant. The clause may stop the tenant from leaving the lease (selling) before the end of their term. Clause details
Reg Order   If there is more than 1 lease on a piece of land, the Reg order shows the order of registration (which lease came first) Lease 1 (DD/MM/YY), Lease 2 (DD/MM/YY)
Price paid  Price paid for the land 1000000
