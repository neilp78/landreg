Data quality (where HM Land Registry gets the data)

Where does the information come from?

The information comes from:

  • citizens
  • corporate bodies
  • conveyancers
  • deeds
  • other documents

Inconsistencies in the data

HM Land Registry data comes from different sources. This means that not everything is written the same way. For example, one address may say ’24 The Willows’ and another might be written as ‘Field south of N14’.

You may also see:

  • spelling errors or typos
  • descriptions instead of addresses (land being developed may not have an address)
  • partial postcodes or no postcode at all
  • mistakes in company registration numbers
  • no data for date of registration
  • no data for company registration number
  • 2 different companies with the same registration number
  • incorrect data due to human error

These inconsistencies are usually either the result of human error (mistakes and typos) or HM Land Registry simply does not have the data. For example, companies who bought a property before 1996 did not need to tell us their registration number.

Think you’ve found a mistake in HM Land Registry data? Contact HM Land Registry:example@hmlandregistry.gov.uk

Included

What is included?

This data shows every (completed) record HM Land Registry has about property-owning companies registered in the UK.

Not included

What is not included?<./h3>

HM Land Registry data does not show:

  • pending applications (we can’t show you a record while it is being written)
  • land or property owned by private individuals, charities or overseas companies
  • company registration numbers before 1996 (unless a company gave theirs voluntarily)

Sometimes we cannot publish data from a new or updated record. For example, if a private individual is the new owner of 1 Smith Street, you will not see the owner’s identity.

HM Land Registry can only show you the information we are given. For example:

  • you buy a new flat on 1 January 2018
  • you tell HM Land Registry about this on 3 March 2018
  • HM Land Registry does not know that you are the legal owner until 3 March
  • data downloaded before 3 March will not show you as the owner of your flat
